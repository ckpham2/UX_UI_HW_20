LivingRoom App

Case Study - 2020

Redesign your home with the latest AR technology home furnishing app

Timeline
Role
2 weeks
UX, UI, Visual Design

The Problem

People find it difficult to visualize how furniture would fit their space when shopping for home furnishings. How might we design LivingRoom so that when people shop for furniture online, they can get recommendations for furniture according to the style of their space, visualize the furniture in there, and compare different models in order to get the best price and quality?

The Solution

By using UX/UI Design, user reseatch and AR technology, we created an app that allows users are able to visualize their room's design before committing to buying furniture.

In this case study, we developed our user research plan after first creating a proto-persona, which then allowed us to conduct interviews and a survey for further insight into the user thought process when buying furniture.

We then defined our user persona.

Finally we designed, tested and then iterated our wireframe prototype based on user test results.

User Research

After gathering data from 16 survey responses, we conducted 6 one-on-one interviews with users who provided information into their thought process when purchasing furniture, with a goal to gain insight on how to make furniture shopping a better experience.

“I like looking at the different styles of furniture, I don't like building furniture or moving things, I would rather just buy it, and that's the most fun part.”
“Dimensions of furniture is important, checking to see if it fits into your space. Sometimes for furniture, I still want to see it in front of you.”
“An AR system would be nice so I could put the furniture in my room before hand virtually, color swatches of the color of the furniture would be nice or recommendations on color scheme, furniture or themes would be helpful.”

We synthesized our data from our 6 interviews and survey into sticky notes and organized them into an affinity diagram with 10 categories based on user demographics, pain points, furniture shopping habits, budget, needs and likes. Through doing this it help us create our user persona, Anthony Rodriguez.

Definition & Ideation

We concluded that it would be most impactful if users could create their own room design and inspiration boards and virtually measure their room dimensions to see their furniture in place before going to the store.

We created our user flow based on our user research, feature prioritization matrix and competitor analysis findings. We wanted to make the app to be curated to the user’s preferred style and then for them to easily scan, browse and place furniture to view within their virtually scanned room while having the option to purchase the furniture all within the app.

Testing & Wireframing

Once we had our user flow, we began creating our lo-fi wireframes and developed a user testing plan for our prototype. We had 6 users test our intital prototype. We asked our users to complete 3 tasks.

Our team’s goal was to determine whether LivingRoom functions as intended. Are users able to successfully flow through the app’s feature’s with ease? What are some pain points and frustrations that the user encountered? And how can we improve upon the things they don’t like in the app?

All of our users were able to successfully work through each task. However, they had some suggestions that we could improve upon. For instance, one users suggested back buttons for the user survey portion. Another user recommended clarity in how one might enter room dimensions manually. Upon checkout, one user would have liked to see a review order tab on the final checkout page.

Overall, users liked how much guidance they were given. They also liked seeing progress bars when there were several tasks asked of them.

Definition & Ideation

From our user test results, we iterated our prototype and made improvements to the design to that would help allow the user use our app easily and produced our High Fidelity Prototype.

Key Takeaways

Designing with the user in mind is something I learned the most in the exploration. Keeping in mind the considerations for design that people who may not be familiar with AR technology. While it was challenging, but it allowed us to make more informed design decisions on what helped the user and what did not. This was also one of the first projects I worked on that made me go through the entire UX process from ideation, user research, market analysis, visuals all the way to protoyping. In essence, it allowed me to fully test my UX design process and how to execute it in a timeline while collaborating with designers on my team.
